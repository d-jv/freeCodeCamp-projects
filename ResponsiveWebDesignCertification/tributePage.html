Guido van Rossum

The creator of the Python programming language.

Guido van Rossum is a Dutch programmer best known as the creator of the Python programming language, for which he was the "benevolent dictator for life" (BDFL) until he stepped down from the position in July 2018. He remained a member of the Python Steering Council through 2019, and withdrew from nominations for the 2020 election.


Here's a time line of Guido van Rossum's life:
  • 1956 - Guido van Rossum is born in Haarlem, Netherlands.
  • 1982 - Guido van Rossum earns his master's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science from the University of Amsterdam
  • 1982 - Guido van Rossum joins CWI as a researcher.
  • 1990 - Python is released at CWI.
  • 1991 - From 1991 to 1995, Guido van Rossum worked for the multimedia group at CWI, Netherlands.
  • 1995 - Guido van Rossum joins U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) as a guest researcher and remained there until February 1998
  • 2000 - Guido van Rossum marries Kim Knapp.
  • 2000 - Guido van Rossum joins Zope Corporation and works there until July 2003.
  • 2002 - Van Rossum received the 2001 Award for the Advancement of Free Software from the Free Software Foundation (FSF) for his work on Python.
  • 2003 - Guido van Rossum joins Elemental Security, and works there until December 2005.
  • 2003 - Guido received a NLUUG Award.
  • 2006 - Guido van Rossum joins Google and works there until December 2012.
  • 2006 - Guido was recognized as a Distinguished Engineer by the Association for Computing Machinery.
  • 2013 - Van Rossum started working at the cloud file storage company Dropbox
  • 2018 - Guido van Rossum becomes a Computer History Museum Fellow.
  • 2019 - Van Rossum left Dropbox and officially retired.
  • 2019 - Van Rossum was awarded the honorary title of Dijkstra Fellow by CWI.
  • 2020 - Van Rossum announced that he was coming out of retirement to join the Developer Division at Microsoft
